Proposed Project Title:
Amrita Student Knowledge Sharing Platform
Proposed Project Pillar:
Education and Student Development

1. Why this project and what's its sustainability?
Every year, Amrita students gain valuable knowledge through academics, projects,
internships, research activities, clubs, and placements.
• Much of this information is lost when students graduate.
• Juniors often struggle to find reliable resources and guidance related to courses,
projects, internships, and placement preparation.
• This project aims to create a centralized platform where Amrita students can share and
access knowledge in an organized manner.
• The platform can be continuously updated by future batches, making it sustainable and
useful for years to come.
• It will serve as a long-term knowledge repository for the Amrita student community.

2. What are the benefits to society?
• Promotes a culture of knowledge sharing and peer learning among students.
• Provides equal access to educational and career-related resources.
• Helps first-generation college students who may not have access to personal guidance
networks.
• Encourages collaboration instead of competition.
• Reduces the gap between seniors and juniors by enabling experience sharing.
• Contributes to building a stronger and more informed student community.

3. What are the probable learnings for the team members?
• Full-stack web development and software engineering practices.
• Database management and system design.

• UI/UX design and user-centered development.
• Project planning, teamwork, and leadership skills.
• Requirement analysis through interaction with students and faculty.
• Documentation, testing, and maintenance of software applications.
• Understanding how technology can be used to solve real educational challenges.

4. How will this project help students in your placement process?
• Students can access placement preparation materials shared by seniors.
• Interview experiences from previous placement seasons can be documented and
referred to.
• Internship experiences and guidance can help students prepare earlier.
• Resources for aptitude, coding, technical interviews, and HR interviews can be organized
in one place.
• Students can learn from successful placement journeys of Amrita alumni and seniors.
• Better access to guidance and resources can improve placement readiness and
confidence.
